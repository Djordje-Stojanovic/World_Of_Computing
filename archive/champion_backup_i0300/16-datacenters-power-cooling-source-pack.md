# Chapter 16 Source Pack: Datacenters, Power, and Cooling

Promoted in pass I-0032.

Chapter 16's evidence spine now separates four lanes before prose drafting:

- Demand scale: IEA and LBNL/DOE for global and U.S. data-centre electricity baselines and scenarios.
- Local constraint: IEA, EPRI, and DOE-SEAB for grid bottlenecks, interconnection timing, and geographic concentration.
- Facility mechanics: EPRI and DOE-SEAB for 100-1,000 MW sites, backup power, flexibility, generation/storage, and facility-level cooling.
- Cooling and density caution: Uptime Institute's 2025 operator survey for PUE plateau and rack-density signals.

The safe frame is "speed to power": model labs and cloud companies can demand compute faster than grids, transformers, generation, transmission, cooling systems, and local planning can adapt. The quarantines are equally important: do not turn scenarios into deterministic forecasts, do not treat clean-energy contracts as physical supply mix, and do not claim broad U.S. grid-aware flexible data-centre operation without further evidence.
