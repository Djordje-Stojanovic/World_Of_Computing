# Reader-Addiction Visual Audit - I-0233

This pass asks a different question from the readiness dashboard: not "is the figure sourced?" but "does the figure make the reader turn the page?"

## Finding

The selected visual program has enough substance. Its next reader-addiction gain will come from restraint, sequencing, and emotional job clarity. Every major visual should do one of six things: create shock, raise suspense, produce surprise, deliver delight, clarify a hard mechanism, or return responsibility to the reader.

The weakest visual moments are not the least polished ones. They are the ones whose purpose is merely to show that the research exists.

## Rule

Beauty is not more pictures. A visual earns its place when it changes the reader's state: it makes the reader feel the shock, understand the mechanism, distrust an easy claim, remember a product surface, notice a physical constraint, or want the next page.

Cut, reserve, or move to apparatus any figure that only performs diligence.
