# Data/Corpus Source Exhibit Shortlist - Pass I-0195

This pass turns the I-0179 corpus acquisition package into a ranked exhibit shortlist. The data chapter needs material texture, but the caption contract is strict: open corpus surfaces show public recipes, transformations, documentation, and risks. They do not reveal closed frontier-lab training mixtures, settle legal questions, prove representativeness, or quantify exact shares.

## First-Wave Exhibits

The first wave should be tokenization, Common Crawl/C4 filtering, The Pile/Dolma mixture recipes, deduplication, memorization/extraction, and DataComp-LM. Together they show text becoming tokens, web data becoming a candidate corpus, open mixtures becoming documented recipes, repeated text changing training behavior, leakage risk becoming measurable, and data curation becoming an experimental variable.

Existing Chapter 17 diagrams already carry much of the story: A-0064 through A-0067. The next execution work should add exact page/table/figure provenance and source-card candidates where they sharpen the evidence, not duplicate the existing diagrams.

## Reserves

FineWeb, RefinedWeb, RedPajama, SlimPajama, ROOTS, BookCorpus, long-context sources, a web-dataset family tree, and a books/web/code caution triptych stay in reserve. They should enter final layout only when they add a distinct job: modern web processing, open reconstruction, dataset-page metadata, multilingual governance, documentation debt, inference-time data pressure, or compact overview.

## Caption Firewall

Every corpus caption must name the source type, source date, transformation step, disclosed scope, and blocked claims. Forbidden inferences include closed-model corpus composition, exact training-data shares, legality, consent, representativeness, dataset quality rank, tokenizer economics, model-specific leakage prevalence, synthetic-data share, and solved memory.
