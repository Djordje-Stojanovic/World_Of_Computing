# Source-Excerpt Card Design and Acquisition Package (I-0189)

This pass turns the planned source-card idea into a governed acquisition package rather than a loose invitation to sprinkle quotations through the book. The core artifact is `data/source_excerpt_card_candidates_i0189.tsv`: 28 candidate typographic source cards across canonical papers, system cards, product launches, GTC slides, benchmark methodology, infrastructure reports, corpus documents, tool-use papers, reasoning papers, and trust/failure-mode sources.

The package is deliberately conservative. Most rows prefer paraphrase-first cards with a small source citation, not long pull quotes. When direct wording is useful, each row carries a local quote limit and a provenance requirement: page, section, slide, snapshot, or hash before the card can move into design. This matters most for Chapters 2-5, where the exhibit quota board showed under-illustration and where primary-source texture can help the reader feel the technical lineage without adding another full diagram.

Design contract:

- Use source cards as evidence objects, not epigraphs.
- Show source title, author or organization, date, source ID, and page/section/slide reference.
- Keep blocked claims visible on the card or in the adjacent caption.
- Prefer paraphrase when exact wording is not doing real work.
- Do not count any I-0189 row as an available exhibit until quote extraction, design, manifesting, and render QA are complete.

Highest-value near-term cards:

- SEC-0189-001 through SEC-0189-005 for the Chapter 2-3 pre-Transformer and Transformer pressure chain.
- SEC-0189-006 through SEC-0189-009 for scaling, compute-optimality, GPT-3 prompting, and RLHF.
- SEC-0189-015 through SEC-0189-018 for the GTC-to-grid bridge between Chapters 15 and 16.
- SEC-0189-020 through SEC-0189-022 for corpus/source-supply texture in Chapter 17.
- SEC-0189-023 through SEC-0189-027 for tools, coding agents, reasoning, benchmark discipline, and trust.

The gap register records the remaining blockers: page extraction, system-card context review, dynamic leaderboard snapshots, GTC attribution limits, mutable product docs, multi-source lab compression, infrastructure press-release limits, corpus legality boundaries, cognitive-metaphor risk, and the fact that these are not finished assets yet.
