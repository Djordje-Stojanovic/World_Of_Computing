# Whole-Book Visual Rhythm Board - I-0215

Pass I-0215 converts the selected-100 exhibit matrix into a reader-experience board. The board maps every planned exhibit by chapter slot and classifies each beat as scene, mechanism, evidence, comparison, or source texture.

## Global Mix

The current selected-100 program is visually strong but still diagram-forward:

- Mechanism: 39
- Source texture: 25
- Comparison: 15
- Evidence: 11
- Scene: 10

That mix is acceptable for a technical nonfiction book, but the local rhythm matters more than the global count. The reader should not encounter several same-class exhibits in a row unless the chapter is deliberately building a sequence.

## Monotony Flags

- Chapters 2-5 are under-varied because each currently has one selected exhibit. They need source texture or scene relief only after paper/source-card provenance is ready.
- Chapter 7 is source-texture heavy. Product/interface surfaces should be separated by mechanism or evidence-control captions so the chapter does not read like a brand montage.
- Chapter 13 has a comparison cluster. Methodology and caveat proximity must interrupt leaderboard/price/rank energy.
- Chapter 16 is source-texture heavy. Physical images and GTC/source surfaces need synthesis diagrams and independent constraint framing between them.
- Chapters 17 and 21-24 are mechanism-heavy. The final layout should add source texture, scene framing, or human judgment beats where rights and provenance allow.

## Production Rule

The selected-100 list is not a finished design. Final layout should pass a rhythm gate: no chapter spread gets three same-class exhibits in a row without a deliberate reason, and every dense comparison/evidence beat needs nearby prose or caption relief.
