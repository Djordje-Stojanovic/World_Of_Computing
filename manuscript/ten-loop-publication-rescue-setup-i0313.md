# Ten-Loop Publication Rescue Setup

Date: 2026-05-27

This setup records the direct override that replaces the old post-I-0312 certification queue.

## Baseline Rejected As Final

The current local baseline remains:

`rendered/final_private_i0312/Next-Token-final-private-polished-matter-i0312.pdf`

It is useful as a proof object because it preserves the assembled manuscript, visual layer, and claim/source ledgers. It is not accepted as the final book.

The direct reader review identified these blocking defects:

- The book opens with ChatGPT rather than a chronological Transformer-era spine.
- Reader-facing captions expose project/audit language instead of trade-book captions.
- Blank and sparse pages survive in the rendered proof.
- Chapter visual portfolio sections behave like dumps rather than edited book pages.
- Some pages carry multiple unrelated images.
- The opening pages do not deliver enough chronology, numbers, tables, formulas, benchmarks, or concrete explanatory force.

## New Active Sprint

`GOAL.md` now defines a 10-pass rescue sprint:

- I-0313: bureaucracy purge.
- I-0314: endnotes-only captions and sourcing.
- I-0315: chronological spine rebuild.
- I-0316: date ranges, timelines, timestamps, and cutoff controls.
- I-0317: blank/sparse page repair.
- I-0318: contextual visual placement.
- I-0319: one visual per page and removal of boards/portfolio plates.
- I-0320: numbers, stats, formulas, benchmarks, charts, and tables.
- I-0321: hostile proofread and rendered-page visual QA.
- I-0322: final private publication candidate.

## Acceptance Standard

The next accepted final PDF must read as a finished chronological nonfiction book, not as an internal project artifact. The visible page must carry story, explanation, image, caption, and endnote-style sourcing. Provenance, rights notes, asset IDs, source IDs, checksums, and claim-boundary controls stay in ledgers and endnotes.
