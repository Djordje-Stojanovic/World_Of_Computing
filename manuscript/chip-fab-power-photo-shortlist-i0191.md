# Chip, Fab, Power, and Photo Shortlist (I-0191)

This pass ranks the physical image surfaces that could give the 100-exhibit program real industrial texture without letting photographs do evidentiary work they cannot do. It pulls together the semiconductor supply-chain board, the nuclear/gas/grid power package, and the rights-safe physical-photo hunt into one final-art triage table.

Best first targets:

- A redrawn AI accelerator supply-stack diagram: EUV, foundry/packaging, HBM, substrates, and racks.
- A redrawn advanced-packaging bottleneck cross-section.
- A redrawn Chapter 16 power-response taxonomy.
- Substation/transmission/nuclear/rack photos only after license revision, attribution, and caption blockers are preserved.

The pass also makes three deliberately conservative calls. First, HBM supplier imagery should usually become a source-card or generic stack redraw, not a product-art comparison that implies supplier rank. Second, gas turbines and nuclear plants should be generic physical texture plus attributed source cards unless same-scope site evidence exists. Third, generic cleanrooms, wafers, cooling towers, and exteriors remain reserves: useful texture, but not more important than mechanism visuals.

No new `assets_manifest.tsv` rows were added. These are ranked acquisition and selection decisions, not publication-ready exhibits.
