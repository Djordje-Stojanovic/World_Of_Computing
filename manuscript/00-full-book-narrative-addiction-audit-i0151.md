# Full-Book Narrative Addiction Audit

Status: prose audit, pass I-0151, 2026-05-26.

This pass scores the current 24-chapter manuscript for reader pull: opener, close, central question, scene density, character/company stakes, technical awe moment, and page-turn handoff. It does not rewrite prose. It identifies where the next surgical prose passes should act.

## Executive Finding

The book now has enough substance to reveal a new weakness: many chapters read better after the first screen than on it. Status notes, source notes, visual integration notes, and future-work bullets are doing useful editorial work, but they often occupy the reader-facing opener or close. That makes the manuscript feel like a high-grade dossier even when the underlying prose contains strong narrative machinery.

The strongest current models are:

- Chapter 16, for the "substation" opener and industrial contradiction.
- Chapter 9, for institutional character and the giant-waking image.
- Chapter 8, for "The public saw a chat box. Microsoft saw a workload."
- Chapter 15, for stagecraft that hands off to the loading dock.
- Chapter 23, for fluency-as-failure tension.

The main repair is not more reporting drama. It is page architecture: let the story start first, move source apparatus below the hook, and make the last visible paragraph a handoff instead of a task list.
