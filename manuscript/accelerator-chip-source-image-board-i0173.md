# Accelerator Chip Source-Image Board - Pass I-0173

This pass broadens the hardware visual pipeline beyond NVIDIA without turning Chapter 14 into a spec-sheet tournament. The useful visual question is: what physical or system surface helps a reader see why LLM compute became a chip, memory, interconnect, software, and rack problem?

## Recommended Visual Strategy

The strongest final art is probably not a wall of vendor renders. Two safer and more useful visual grammars emerge from the board:

1. A neutral accelerator taxonomy: GPU, TPU, Trainium, Gaudi, Ascend, and LPU, each shown as a vendor-attributed source lane rather than a ranked claim.
2. A scale ladder from chip to package to board to rack to cluster, using NVIDIA GB200/Vera Rubin, Google TPU pods, AWS Trainium2 systems, Huawei Atlas/Ascend systems, and AMD/Intel accelerator packages as source anchors.

Those two redraw paths would give Chapter 14 balance while keeping Chapter 15 free to analyze NVIDIA's GTC stagecraft and Chapter 16 free to test system rhetoric against power, cooling, networking, and site constraints.

## Priority Image Candidates

Highest priority:

- NVIDIA GB200/Blackwell rack or package surface, paired with existing GTC chronology.
- NVIDIA Vera Rubin page-provenance surfaces already captured from the 2026 keynote deck.
- AMD MI300X/MI350 product-family surface to keep the accelerator race from becoming NVIDIA-only.
- Google TPU family/pod surface to show cloud-owned silicon as a separate path.
- AWS Trainium2 chip/server surface to show hyperscaler-owned training infrastructure.
- Huawei Atlas/Ascend SuperPoD surface for the China/domestic accelerator lane.
- A house-style cross-vendor scale ladder, because it teaches the mechanism with fewer rights and ranking risks.

Medium priority:

- Intel Gaudi 3 product image if a rights-safe press/product surface is available.
- Groq LPU/LPX only as an inference-specialized contrast, not a broad GPU replacement claim.
- H100/Hopper as the historical baseline if Chapter 14 still needs a pre-Blackwell anchor.

## Claim Discipline

The board blocks six temptations: product renders as deployment proof, specs as rankings, roadmaps as shipped history, China images as export-control conclusions, cloud custom silicon as usage-share evidence, and cross-vendor visuals as implicit performance comparisons.

The final captions should favor verbs like "presented," "positioned," "framed," and "shown in vendor materials." They should avoid "faster," "cheaper," "dominant," "available," "deployed," and "won" unless a same-scope evidence row exists.
