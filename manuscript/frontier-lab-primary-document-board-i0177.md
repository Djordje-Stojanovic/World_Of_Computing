# Frontier Lab Primary-Document Acquisition Board (I-0177)

This pass turns the lab landscape into a source-acquisition problem. The book already had strong rows for OpenAI, Anthropic, Google DeepMind, Meta, Mistral, Qwen, DeepSeek, GLM, Kimi k1.5, xAI's early Grok sources, and NVIDIA's Nemotron/open-model lane. The weak spot was balance: some labs had durable local captures and technical reports, while others existed only as remembered names, secondary summaries, or tempting logos.

The new board in `data/frontier_lab_primary_document_board_i0177.tsv` treats each lab as a source surface with a mechanism role. OpenAI is an API/model-doc/source-policy lane. Anthropic is a system-card and coding-agent lane. Google DeepMind is a product-post plus model-card lane. Meta is an open-weight release-strategy lane. Mistral, Cohere, and AI21 become mechanism-diversity lanes: efficient/open European models, enterprise RAG/tool-use, and hybrid SSM-Transformer architecture. Qwen, DeepSeek, GLM/Z.ai, Kimi/Moonshot, MiniMax, Baidu, Tencent, Xiaomi MiMo, and StepFun become Chinese/open/frontier-source lanes with explicit gaps instead of a logo parade.

The screenshot queue in `data/frontier_lab_screenshot_priority_i0177.tsv` prioritizes pages that can carry claims visually: system-card covers, model-card covers, launch tables, arXiv abstract pages, official technical-report pages, and dynamic documentation surfaces. These are private-use acquisition targets, not publish-ready images. Vendor benchmark charts should be redrawn or treated as quotation objects only after rights and methodology review.

The gap register in `data/frontier_lab_gap_register_i0177.tsv` is just as important as the supported rows. xAI still needs durable capture because official pages were browser-readable but shell capture failed in an earlier pass. GLM/Z.ai newer-family claims, Qwen 3.5/3.6, DeepSeek V4-era claims, and StepFun chronology are blocked until exact official surfaces exist. Baidu, Tencent, MiniMax, and Xiaomi now have stronger source footholds, but most claims beyond release/mechanism still require extraction and supporting evidence.

The operating rule from this pass is simple: a frontier lab earns space in the book through a primary document, model card, technical report, official docs page, or captured screenshot that explains a mechanism under the cutoff. Logos, national category, and leaderboard memory are not evidence.
