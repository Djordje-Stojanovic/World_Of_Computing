# Selected-100 Caption Excellence Pass - I-0216

Pass I-0216 audits every selected-100 exhibit caption against a stricter publication contract: each caption should teach one thing, fit its chapter beat, include a provenance hook, and avoid unsupported claim drift.

## Findings

The derived QA table covers all 100 selected exhibits. Under the strict screen:

- 33 captions currently pass the caption contract.
- 50 rows need claim-language review. Many of these flags are expected because good captions already mention blocked terms such as adoption, rank, safety, cost, or productivity; the next edit must make sure the blocker language is explicit.
- 22 rows need rights/private-use or source-surface cue reinforcement.
- 6 rows are long enough that final layout should compress the caption body and move caveats into nearby notes.

This pass does not rewrite the final captions yet. It creates the caption QA surface that should guide I-0217 attribution work and later render QA.

## Caption Rule

Final caption body: one teaching sentence plus one provenance or caveat hook. Dense blocker language belongs in a nearby source note when the page can preserve proximity.

The most dangerous captions are not the weakest-looking captions; they are the beautiful concrete ones. Product screenshots, GTC slides, photos, and benchmark charts can make unsupported adoption, revenue, safety, deployment, live-rank, price-quality, productivity, or autonomy claims feel true unless the caption blocks the leap at the point of use.
