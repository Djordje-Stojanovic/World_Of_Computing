# Nuclear, Gas, Grid Power Source-Image Package - Pass I-0176

This pass gives Chapter 16 a disciplined power-image menu. The book needs physical stakes: nuclear restarts, SMR agreements, gas generation, substations, transmission, batteries, and interconnection planning. But power visuals are among the easiest images to overread. A plant photo or utility press release cannot prove where electrons went, what model used them, what the hourly fuel mix was, or whether the buildout solved anything.

## Best Final-Art Path

The strongest final exhibit is a power-response taxonomy, not a heroic plant photo. It should place six responses side by side:

1. Nuclear PPAs and restart proposals.
2. Advanced nuclear / SMR agreements.
3. Gas generation and on-site generation.
4. Transmission and interconnection upgrades.
5. Storage and flexibility.
6. Clean procurement versus physical supply.

Each cell should have a source label and a blocked-claim label. The visual job is to show that AI compute turns into a power-planning problem with many imperfect responses.

## Source Roles

Existing IEA, LBNL, DOE, EPRI, and Uptime rows remain the general evidence spine. The new rows add project-level texture:

- Constellation/Microsoft: nuclear restart/PPA as procurement and future-delivery evidence, not proof of delivered power.
- Google/Kairos: advanced nuclear agreement as a future-dated supply strategy, not proof of operating SMRs.
- Constellation/Meta: existing nuclear plant output contracted through a PPA, not a site-specific fuel-mix claim.
- Amazon/Susquehanna/FERC: a governance and interconnection conflict lane that needs primary order capture before final art.
- Entergy/Meta Richland Parish: utility infrastructure responding to a large data-center project, with gas-plant counts and bill/emissions effects kept blocked unless primary filings are captured.

## Visual Priorities

The safest near-term visual is a source-card matrix. Physical photos should be generic and rights-safe first: substation, transmission, battery storage, cooling or turbine equipment. Named-site photos require more evidence because the caption can accidentally imply operational status or fuel delivery.

The existing Chapter 16 visuals already carry the mechanism: power-to-token flow, interconnection queue, cooling/rack density, and clean procurement versus physical supply. This package adds the source-image menu that can make those mechanisms feel embodied without weakening truth.

## Drop Rules

Drop any image that invites a reader to infer energy-per-token, site fuel mix, exact MW, delivered nuclear power, solved grid access, emissions totals, local customer bill effects, or customer workloads. Keep the power story as a capacity-gate story: necessary, physical, contested, and still unresolved by the cutoff.
