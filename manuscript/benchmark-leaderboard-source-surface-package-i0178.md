# Benchmark and Leaderboard Source-Surface Package (I-0178)

Benchmarks are useful in this book only when the reader can see what kind of measurement is being made. This pass adds a benchmark source-surface board, caption contracts, and a gap register so Chapter 13 and the code/reasoning/trust chapters can use ranking drama without turning a leaderboard into a crown.

The core rule is that every benchmark score needs a contract. Arena rows require category, split, publication date, snapshot, and uncertainty. SWE-bench and Terminal-Bench require scaffold, tools, task variant, attempts, pass criteria, and run date. Artificial Analysis rows require methodology version, evaluation basket, access date, and metric definitions. Static academic benchmarks such as MMLU, GPQA, MATH, HumanEval, and TruthfulQA require benchmark variant, task scope, prompting/scoring rule, and model/date.

The new source rows expand the benchmark surface beyond the existing Arena, SWE-bench, LiveCodeBench, HumanEval, and TruthfulQA entries. Artificial Analysis is treated as a dynamic methodology and measurement surface, not as a stable source of permanent rank. Terminal-Bench enters as an agent/terminal harness source. MMLU, GPQA, and MATH enter as benchmark genealogy surfaces for the reasoning and trust chapters.

The gap register keeps the dangerous claims visible. Live leaderboards need snapshots. Vendor-reported Claude 4 SWE-bench and Terminal-Bench numbers remain chart-blocked until C-0013 is normalized against benchmark variant, scaffold, tools, task setup, and independent source surfaces. Benchmark screenshots remain private-use acquisition targets unless rights, hashes, viewports, and redraw decisions are recorded.
