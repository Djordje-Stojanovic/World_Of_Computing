# Commercial Publishing Package

Pass I-0167 turns the book's current intellectual position into a commercial package without pretending the product is launch-ready.

## Core Package

Keep the working title:

**Next Token: The Race to Build the Machines That Learned Language, Code, and Computing**

The strongest shorter subtitle to test later is:

**How Language Models Became the New Computing Interface**

Draft jacket promise:

Every age of computing gets a new command surface. Ours arrived as a box of text that could answer, invent, hallucinate, code, search, call tools, and ask for more power. **Next Token** tells the story of how that happened.

This is a serious, illustrated history of large language models through May 24, 2026: embeddings, attention, Transformers, scaling, GPT-3, ChatGPT, Gemini, Claude, Llama, Qwen, DeepSeek, Claude Code, benchmarks, CUDA, GTC 2026, datacenters, data, inference economics, and trust.

## Shelf Position

Primary comps:

- **Chip War** for industrial and hardware seriousness.
- **The Prize** for infrastructure-scale ambition.
- Walter Isaacson's computing biographies for broad computing-history readability.
- **The Coming Wave** as a current AI shelf contrast: this book should be less prophecy and more machinery.

## KDP-Aware Metadata

Current KDP guidance matters. Title and subtitle together must stay under the metadata limit, descriptions should not contain reviews, testimonials, prices, availability, URLs, promotional dates, or keyword stuffing, and KDP currently lets authors select up to three categories based on primary audience and marketplace. eBook preorders are available, but paperback preorder is not the same thing; paperback can use a release date. KDP's price/royalty system also depends on marketplace, royalty option, VAT/tax, delivery costs, file size, and print cost.

Recommended category lanes:

- Technology / Artificial Intelligence
- Business & Money / Industries / Computers & Technology
- Computers & Technology / History & Culture

Backend keyword draft:

`next token`; `large language models`; `ChatGPT history`; `artificial intelligence history`; `generative AI business`; `AI infrastructure`; `coding agents`

## Pricing And Preorder

Initial price tests:

- Ebook: `$9.99` if the final file supports the 70% royalty economics.
- Paperback: `$24.99-$29.99` depending on page count, trim, color, and print cost.
- Hardcover: `$34.99-$39.99` only if the visual program justifies a premium edition.

Current preorder verdict: **no preorder yet**.

Reason: I-0166 measured the canonical primary-file spine at 98,529 words; I-0164 still shows a 10-exhibit floor gap; source-density repairs, quote permissions, Chapter 12 structure, unsupported claims, and full-book PDF render QA remain open. Marketing pressure should not create a release date the manuscript cannot truthfully meet.

## Launch Shape

The launch should happen in six phases: product repair, package freeze, ARC preparation, review/blurb round, preorder/direct-launch decision, and post-launch optimization. The key commercial choice is restraint: build enough reader pull to sell, but do not let category gaming, hype language, or premature preorder mechanics outrun the book's truth standard.
